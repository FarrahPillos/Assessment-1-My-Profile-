Project Ideas

Overview

This project will be called "My Medical Chart", it is a Personalised on the go Medical chart of every individual on their smartphones. The target client of this application will be anyone who wants to have access with their medical chart, mostly older people, people with disability and medical personnel. This application will enable medical practitioners and Allied health to edit, upload documents to the patient's medical records anytime. Hence, Medical records will be accessible to all clients. Patient's privacy will be the top priority of this application, without consent from the patient, changes to the application are not allowed.


Motivation

According to Meticulous Research, an expected Compound Annual Growth rate at 13.8% in the healthcare IT market will reach a value of $511 billion between 2017 and 2019 (Dyzma 2020). Furthermore, personal access to medical records is a struggle, costly and time-consuming (Krumholz 2019). In addition, dealing with a patient's medical chart has an associated problem in which patient's medical records are stored across different sectors such as public hospitals, and private general medical practices (Senate Community Affairs Committees). Getting medical information from the patient is sometimes frustrating due to the inability of the patient to remember information. The frustrations of getting information from different locations and sources motivate me to propose this application as well as the predicted increase in the use of technology in the healthcare setting.


Description

The application will be available and downloaded through smartphones and computers. The application will prompt the client to do biometrics, such as facial recognition or a number code. If successful to open the application, the main page of the application will give the reader the main information of the patient such as full name, Birthdate, Age, Height and weight, Allergy, Implants available such as a pacemaker, and major disease of the patient like diabetes. On the top left portion of the page, a button called chart will be available; this will let the reader view the whole medical records of the patient. On the bottom left portion of the page, an emergency button will be available for the patient to press as soon as possible if emergency assistance is needed.

Once on the page of the chart, the application will let the viewer select a different category such as Medical History, Medications list, Bloods laboratory result, Imaging results, Clinical visits, Medical Personnel notes, Medical observations like temperature, pulse rate, blood pressure and blood sugar logs, and appointment tabs will be available. Upon selecting those buttons, the reader will view from recent logs up to old files. For the medical practitioner or allied health, the application will be available to them to view if they will log in using their company code and employee number. However, medical professionals are not allowed to edit the My Medical Chart without patient consent. In the event, where the medical practitioner needs to update the "My Medical Chart" of a patient, the practitioner must acquire consent from the patient. A medical practitioner will log in to the app with company code and employees identification number. After inputting the company code and employee number, the patient will be prompted with a notification that allows another person to access its My Medical Chart. Once acknowledged, the practitioner will be able to update patient's medical records like uploading imaging results, uploading blood results and write medical instructions to the medical personnel notes tab. Sometimes in times of emergency and the patient is not able to consent the practitioner's ability to edit the My Medical Chart, the option will be given to next of kin to give consent on behalf of the patient. The next of kin will have the power to consent to the health team.

For the emergency button, after downloading the application, the patient will need to input basic information such as full name, birth date, height and weight. However, there will be an option for the patient wanting to add a next of kin to consent on its behalf accessing the My Medical Chart in case of emergency.

On the negative part of this application, an increased risk of personal data exploitation might happen if the patient is not careful in securing the app. Secondly, the health care team acts unprofessionally by not following patients' privacy rights. In addition, a possibility to forget the codes. Hence, patients have an option to use biometrics instead.


Tools and Technologies

For the tools and software for this application, it needs a computer and smartphones with an internet connection, wifi, and a bluetooth connectivity for transferring and uploading files. It also needs programming languages such as Android SDK, SQL Database, web Server and HTML. Thus, in enabling this application "My Medical Chart" to access Electronic Health Records (EHR) software. Electronic Health Records software is a centralised database used by hospitals and clinics to store medical history, lab results, treatment plans, medications and even patient billing information (The Top 20 Most Popular EMR Software Solutions | Capterra, 2020).


Skills Required

Skills necessary for this project include problem-solving skills, use of different programming languages, excellent skills in time and organisational management, skills in detail and accuracy, teamwork skills and mostly self-development skills (Target Jobs 2020). Moreover, a knowledge of the current security policies implemented by the Australian Government in accessing databases on electronic health records is also needed to operate the project legally. At the time being, my knowledge and skills in Information Technology are far from reality in doing this project. On top of that, the cost of the software and legal processing is not feasible.


Outcome

Patients' ability to provide health records anytime will give them fast medical attention in the event they need it the most such as if a patient is overseas or visiting a health facility without a medical record software access. As a health worker, accessing a patient's document in an easy manner will improve the efficiency of giving care to patients. Moreover, medical practitioners will be on track with the patient's current medical status. Thus, enable medical practitioners to provide proper treatment for the patient. This application will benefit both the patient and the healthcare team.
